Guidance for Standards I-VII

Created for AlchemistsAcademy by MacLane Wilkison

Introduction

The following is a discussion of the recommended procedures for compliance for each of the relevent Standards

Standard I - Professionalims

A. Knowledge of the Law

  • Members and Candidates should:
    1. Stay informed
    2. Review procedures
    3. Maintain current files
    4. Make reasonable efforts to understand applicable laws
    5. Seek the advice of legal counsel when in doubt
  • Firms should:
    1. Develop and/or adopt a code of ethics
    2. Provide information on applicable laws
    3. Establish procedures for reporting violations

B. Independence and Objectivity

  • Firms should:
    1. Protect the integrity of opinions
    2. Create a restricted list
    3. Restrict special cost arrangements
    4. Limit gifts
    5. Restrict employee investments
    6. Review procedures
    7. Establish an independence policy
    8. Appoint an officer with oversight responsibilities

C. Misrepresentation

  • Members and Candidates should:
    1. Publish only factual presentations
    2. Prepare a summary of his qualifications and experience
    3. Verify third-party information
    4. Maintain and refresh informmation contained on webpages
    5. Avoid plagiarism by (1) maintaining copies, attributing quotations, and (3) attributing summaries

D. Misconduct

  • Firms should:
    1. Develop a code of ethics
    2. Develop a list of potential violations and associated disciplinary actions
    3. Verify references of potential employees

Standard II - Integrity of Capital Markets

A. Material Nonpublic Information

  • Members and firms should:
    1. Achieve public dissemination of material information
    2. Adopt compliance procedures
    3. Adopt discolsure procedures
    4. Issure press realeases
    5. Establish firewalls to prevent release of MNPI
    6. Monitor appropriate interdepartmental communication
    7. Establish physical separation of departments
    8. Prevent overlap of personnel between departments
    9. Establish a reporting system
    10. Implement personal trading limitaitons
    11. Maintain records
    12. Implement proprietary trading procedures
    13. Disseminate compliance policies and guidelines

B. Market Manipulation

  • Members and firms should:
    1. Avoid the dissemination of false or misleading information
    2. Avoid transactions that deceive or would be likely to mislead market participants by distorting the price-setting mechanism of financial instruments

Standard III - Duties to Clients

A. Loyalty, Prudence, and Care

  • Members should:
    1. Provide client's with regular account information
    2. Receive client approval for any questionable matters

A. Loyalty, Prudence, and Care (cont'd)

  • Firms should:
    1. Follow all applicable rules and laws
    2. Establish the investment objectives of the client
    3. Consider all the information when taking actions
    4. Diversify
    5. Carry out regular reviews
    6. Deal fairly with all clients with respect to investment actions
    7. Disclose conflicts of interest
    8. Disclose compensation arrangements
    9. Vote proxies
    10. Maintain confidentiality
    11. Seek best execution
    12. Place client interests first

B. Fair Dealing

  • When dealing with recommendations firms should:
    1. Limit the number of people involved
    2. Shorten the time frame between decision and dissemination
    3. Publish guidelines for pre-dissemination
    4. Strive for simultaneous dissemination
    5. Maintain a list of clients and their holdings
    6. Develop and document trade allocation procedures
  • Firms should also:
    1. Disclose trade allocation procedures
    2. Systematically review accounts
    3. Disclose service levels

C. Suitability

  • Members should:
    1. Create a written IPS for clients
    2. Regularly update clients' IPS
  • Firms should:
    1. Develop suitability test policies

D. Performance Presentation

  • Firms should comply with GIPS standards
  • If not compliant, Members should:
    1. Consider the knowledge and sophistication of the audience to whom a presentation is addressed
    2. Present the performance of the weighted composite of similar portfolios rather than a single representative account
    3. Include terminated accounts in performance history
    4. Include appropriate disclosures explaining reported results
    5. Maintain data and records used to calculate performance results

E. Preservation of Confidentiality

  • Members should:
    1. Avoid disclosing any information received from a client except to authorized fellow employees who are also working for the client or to the appropriate authorities in the event of illegal activity on the part of the client

Standard IV - Duties to Employers

A. Loyalty

  • Firms should:
    1. Establish a clear competition policy
    2. Establish a clear termination policy
    3. Establish incident-reporting procedures
    4. Classify employees

B. Additional Compensation Arrangements

  • Members should:
    1. Make immediate written disclosures to their employer describing any compensation they propse to receive for services in addition to the compensation received from the employer

C. Responsibilities of Supervisors

  • Firms should:
    1. Establish compliance procedures for their code of ethics
    2. Ensure adequate compliance procedures

Standard V - Investment Analysis, Recommendations, and Actions

A. Diligence and Reasonable Basis

  • Firms should:
    1. Establish policies requiring investment analyses to have a reasonable and adequate basis
    2. Develop detailed, written guidance for due diligence procedures
    3. Develop measurable criteria for assessing resarch quality
    4. Develop measurable criteria for assessing outside providers
    5. Adopt a standardized set of criteria for evaluating the adequacy of external advisers

B. Communication with Clients and Prospective Clients

  • Members should:
    1. Maintain records indicating the nature of their research and should be able to supply additional information to the client, upon request

C. Record Retention

  • Firms and Members should:
    1. Maintain records that support investment actions
    2. Archive research notes and other documents that support their current investment-related communications

Standard VI - Conflicts of Interest

A. Disclosure of Conflicts

  • Members should:
    1. Disclose special compensation arrangements with their employer that might conflict with client interests
  • Firms should:
    1. Include information on compensation packages in firms' promotional literature

B. Priority of Transactions

  • Firms should:
    1. Limit participation in equity IPOs
    2. Restrict participation in private placements
    3. Establish blackout/restricted periods
    4. Establish reporting requirements and procedures
    5. Disclose policies relating to personal investing

C. Referral Fees

  • Firms should:
    1. Develop procedures related to referral fees
    2. Provide notification to clients of approved referral fee programs

Standard VII - Responsibilities as a CFA Insitute Member or Candidate

A. Conduct as Members and Candidates in the CFA Program

  • Members/Candidates should:
    1. Avoid any impropriety related to the CFA examination
    2. Avoid disclosing details related to the CFA examination
  • It is permissible to express an opinion regarding the CFA Program or Institute

Reference to the CFA Institute, the CFA Designation, and the CFA Program

  • Reference the CFA Institute, Designation, and Program in the correct manner
