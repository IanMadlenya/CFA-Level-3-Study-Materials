Managing Individual Investor Portfolios

Created for AlchemistsAcademy by MacLane Wilkison

Investor Characteristics

  • Situational profiling
    • Source of wealth - active (e.g. entrepreneurship) vs. passive
    • Measure of wealth
    • Stage of life-cycle - foundation (learning), accumulation (earning), maintenance, distribution
  • Psychological profiling
    • Cautious - loss-averse
    • Methodical - analytical, disciplined, conservative
    • Spontaneous - trade often and emotionally
    • Individualist
Situational profiling helps to establish an investor's basic philosophy and preferences. Psychological profiling identifies the thought processes, biases, and behaviors that influence an individuals risk and return attitudes.

Investment Policy Statement

  1. Set return and risk objectives
    • Return objective - driven by annual spending (income) and long-term savings (growth) goals
    • Risk objective - driven by both ability and willingness to take risk
  2. Identify constraints
    • Liquidity - portfolio (transaction costs, price volatility) and investor requirements (ongoing expenses, emergency reserves, negative liqudity events)
    • Time horizon - single- or multi-stage and life-cycle classifications
    • Taxes - income, capital gains, wealth transfer, property
    • Legal and regulatory environment
    • Unique circumstances

Asset Allocation Process

  1. Determine the asset allocations that meet the investor's return requirements
  2. Eliminate asset allocations that fail to meet quantitative risk objectives or are otherwise inconsistent with the investor's risk tolerance
  3. Eliminate asset allocations that fail to satisfy the investor's state constraints
  4. Evaluate the expected risk-adjusted performance and diversification attributes of each allocation and select the most attractive one
