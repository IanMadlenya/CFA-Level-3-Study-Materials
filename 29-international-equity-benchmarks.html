International Equity Benchmarks

Created for AlchemistsAcademy by MacLane Wilkison

Introduction

  • International equity benchmarks differ from US ones in that:
    • Float adjustments are much more important
    • Markets are often segmented into developed and emerging categories
    • Investors must account for currencies and construct both local- and domestic-currency benchmarks

Trade-offs in Constructing International Indices

  1. Breadth vs. investability
  2. Liquidity and crossing opportunities vs. index reconstitution effects
  3. Precise float adjustment vs. transaction costs from rebalancing
  4. Objectivity and transparency vs. judgment
1) Small-cap and closely held international stocks are often very illiquid and, thus, are typically excluded from indices; 2) Crossing is the process by which an investment manager matches his own clients' buy/sell orders without a broker. Index reconstitution refers to upward (downward) price pressure on securities that are included (deleted) from an index; 3) Float adjustments have a large effect on constituent weights and frequently revised adjustments can drive up transaction costs via rebalancing; 4) Objective and clearly stated rules for index construction enable users to predict what will be in the benchmark and trade more effectively
