Foreign Currency Risk

  • Transaction exposure
  • Translation exposure
  • Economic exposure
  • Foreign currency receipt
  • Foreign currency payment
Transaction exposure is the risk associated with a foreign exchange rate on a specific business transaction. Translation exposure is the risk associated with the conversion of foreign financial statements into domestic currency. Foreign currenct payments and receipts are exposed to risk associated with changes in the relative attractiveness of products and services offered for sale, arising out of the competitive effects of changes in exchange rates. In the case of a receipt, an investor can hedge by selling the currency forward and locking-in price. In the case of a payment, an investor can go long a currency forward. If an investor hedges against forward equity returns, a foreign asset portfolio will earn the foreign risk-free rate. If the investor also hedges the exchange rate, they will earn the domestic risk-free rate.
