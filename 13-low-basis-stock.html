Low-Basis Stock

Created for AlchemistsAcademy by MacLane Wilkison

Introduction

Definition: Stock that has a much higher market value than its tax cost

  • Basis - refers to the price used for the calculation of capital gains

Understanding Stock risk

  • Market(systematic) risk vs. specific risk
  • Three stages of equity holding life
    1. Entrepreneurial stage - no diversification; maximum investment in one, high-potential idea
    2. Executive stage - somewhat diversified; extent of diversification linked to the degree of control the individual has over the company's performance
    3. Investor stage - high diversification
Market risk affects all securities in the same class while specific risk relates to each particular security in isolation.

The Four Stages of Specific Stock Risk

Reducing a Concentrated Exposure

  • Outright sale
  • Exchange funds
  • Completion portfolios
    • Single-asset class vs. multi-asset class completion portfolios
  • Hedging strategies
There are several option available to an investor seeking to reduce her exposure to a given holding. An outright sale is the simplest and most straightforward option but it can trigger a tax liability upon realization of any capital gains. Exchange funds are created when an advisor brings together several individuals, each with a different concentrated position(s), and pools their assets. Completion portfolios allow investors to create a portfolio that behaves like a desired index or basket by combining their low-basis position with some or all of his additional liquid assets. This can be accomplished whether the low-basis position is concentrated in one single asset or in a group of similar assets. There are several hedging strategies availabe to investors looking to diversify their exposure.
